Clinical education faces unprecedented challenges including growing shortages of qualified supervisors, limited access to diverse patient encounters, and inconsistent feedback quality that collectively threaten the development of competent healthcare professionals. The complex theory-practice gap in medical training has been further exacerbated by recent global disruptions, creating urgent need for scalable, high-quality educational solutions. We introduce CLARA (Clinical Listening and Reasoning Assistant), a novel AI-powered multimodal education system that integrates electronic health records, voice interaction, and medical imaging through a mixture-of-experts architecture to simulate authentic clinical encounters. The system employs OpenAI Whisper for automatic speech recognition, a three-tier clinical NER pipeline (transformer-based bert-base-uncased_clinical-ner -> spaCy -> regex fallback) for medical natural language processing, and librosa for acoustic feature extraction (pitch, tempo, jitter, pause duration), unified by a semantic alignment engine that provides real-time feedback on communication skills, diagnostic reasoning, and treatment planning. LLM-enhanced feedback is powered by Deepseek R1 for clinical reasoning and Qwen3 for structured analysis via OpenRouter, with full rule-based fallback for offline deployment. CLARA offers unprecedented educational access through 24/7 availability, transparent clinical reasoning development that makes expert thought processes visible to learners, and standardized feedback delivery that ensures consistent high-quality guidance regardless of geographic location or institutional resources. Prototype validation on MIMIC-IV (n=999 expert notes, 91.59% NER confidence) and VietMed (n=5 trainee sessions, 85 audio clips) confirms an 11x expert-trainee reasoning gap, demonstrating the system's ability to quantify and target clinically significant performance deficits. By providing adaptive learning capabilities that personalize educational pathways and seamless integration with existing educational frameworks, CLARA represents a transformative approach to preparing future physicians for the complexities of modern healthcare while complementing rather than replacing human clinical mentorship.
